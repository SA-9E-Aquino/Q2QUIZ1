Ethereum

Information about Ethereum:

Ethereum is a de-centralized blockchain with smart contract functionality also called as "Ether" or in abbreviation "ETH" and is second to only to bitcoin in market capitalization and is an open-source software.

Ethereum was planned by programmer Vitalik Buterin in 2013, other founders also include Gavin Wood, Charles Hoskinson, Anthony Di lorio, and Joseph Lubin.

Ethereum went live on July 30, 2015.

Ethereum is considered to be unique in way by many due to it's peer-to-peer network which allows anyone to deploy permanent and immutable decentralized applications, where users can interact, there was also Decentralized finance applications that provide monetary contracts. Ethereum also allows users to create and exchange NFTS (Non-fungible tokens) that are tied to digital assets like images.

Binance

Information about Binance:

Binance is a cryptocurrency that has the largest cryptocurrency exchange when it comes to daily trading volume of crytocurrencies.

Binance was founded and created in 2017 by Changpeng Zhao, who was a developer who had experience creating high-frequency trading software prior to his foundig of Binance.

Binance was initially based in China, however they decided to move to Japan after the Chinese government restricted cryptocurrency companies. Binance then subsequently left Japan for Malta and currently has no official Headquarters.

Binance has a rough history because it's facing lawsuits and challenges from authorities, thus Binance has been restriced to operate and banned from many countries like the United Kingdom.

Tether

Information about Tether:

Tether is a cryptocurrency stable coin launced by Tether Limited Inc. in 2014.

Tether was created when J.R. Willett published a whitepaper that described the possibility of building new cryptocurrencies on top of the bitcoin blockchain. Willett went on to help implement this idea in cryptocurrency, Mastercoin to promote this idea. The Mastercoin protocol would later become the technological foundation of the Tether cryptocurrency.

It was orginally named "Realcoin" when it was announced in July 2014 by the co-founders. It would be later called "Tether" on November 20, 2014.
